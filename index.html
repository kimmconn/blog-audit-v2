The problem

There are so many tools to help you create new content these days. But what about tools to help you most effectively update the old stuff?

You've got 300+ posts sitting on your blog. Which ones are actually losing you money?

Be honest — you don't know. Nobody does, once you're past a few hundred posts. Traffic dips on some of them, a few keep slipping down the rankings, and you've got a vague feeling that "something" needs updating. But actually opening Google Analytics and figuring out which post, out of hundreds, deserves your afternoon? That's where most bloggers just... stop. Or choose a random one and hope for the best.

🔍

You try to dive into Search Console data, but give up when it gets too complicated

🎯

You pour hours into a post that was already ranking fine, while a real money-loser sits untouched on page 2

💰

You check your highest-RPM posts on your ad network, but there's no way to know if you're actually picking the best ones to fix

💬

And yes, we love Yoast too — but "keep your content fresh" is where the advice stops. It'll remind you that old posts need updating. It won't tell you which of your 400 posts to open first, or why one dying post might be worth more to fix than the other ten combined.

The fix

Bloupi checks the SEO ranking signals for each post on your blog, and hands you a list of which ones to update based on urgency
